About Miriam Peretz

Miriam Peretz is an Israeli educator, author, and public figure who serves as an inspiration to many through her extraordinary life story and dedication to promoting values of education, giving, and love for the country. She was born in Morocco in 1954 and immigrated to Israel as a child. For many years, she worked as a teacher and school principal, driven by her belief in the power of education to shape future generations. Miriam tragically lost her two sons, Lieutenant Uriel and Major Eliraz Peretz, who fell in military service. Despite her immense grief, she chose to dedicate her life to strengthening the spirit of the nation and educating for hope, unity, and leadership. Her lectures and books touch the hearts of many and impact diverse audiences across the country. In recognition of her contributions to society and the state, she was awarded the Israel Prize in 2018. Her interests include education, writing, public speaking, and community empowerment, and she continues to work tirelessly to preserve her sonsâ€™ legacy and share her love for the people and the country.

Key Differences Between Web1, Web2, and Web3

  • Web1: A read-only web, where users can only consume information. The websites are static, and the interaction is limited to viewing content.
  • Web2: Focuses on user-generated content, allowing interactivity and social features. Websites are dynamic and personalized, enabling users to engage with content and share it easily.
  • Web3: A decentralized web, leveraging blockchain technology. Web3 gives users control over their data, prioritizing privacy, security, and user ownership. It focuses on peer-to-peer interactions without intermediaries.

Use Case: "Side Effects of Medications"

Web1

In Web1, searching for "side effects of medications" would lead you to a static webpage with general, read-only information. You'd only be able to read the content, and there wouldn't be any interactive elements, comments, or updates.

Web2

On Web2, the search results would be dynamic, showing user-generated content such as forums, product reviews, and medical blogs. You might see personalized content or suggestions based on your search history, and you could interact with other users through comments or sharing.

Web3

In Web3, the search results would come from decentralized databases or blockchain platforms. Information would be more transparent, and you might be able to access peer-reviewed or verified data. User privacy and data control would be prioritized, ensuring that the content you interact with is secure and reliable.
