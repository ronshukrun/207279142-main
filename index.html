About Miriam Peretz

Miriam Peretz is an Israeli educator, author, and public figure who serves as an inspiration to many through her extraordinary life story and dedication to promoting values of education, giving, and love for the country. She was born in Morocco in 1954 and immigrated to Israel as a child. For many years, she worked as a teacher and school principal, driven by her belief in the power of education to shape future generations. Miriam tragically lost her two sons, Lieutenant Uriel and Major Eliraz Peretz, who fell in military service. Despite her immense grief, she chose to dedicate her life to strengthening the spirit of the nation and educating for hope, unity, and leadership. Her lectures and books touch the hearts of many and impact diverse audiences across the country. In recognition of her contributions to society and the state, she was awarded the Israel Prize in 2018. Her interests include education, writing, public speaking, and community empowerment, and she continues to work tirelessly to preserve her sons’ legacy and share her love for the people and the country.

"Miriam Peretz, Laureate of the Israel Prize"
The Israel Prize ceremony is one of the most prestigious events in the country, honoring individuals who have made significant contributions to Israeli society. Miriam Peretz, laureate of the Israel Prize, is a symbol of courage. After losing her two sons in Israel's wars, she became a role model, leading with values of hope, resilience, and love for the land.


"The Power of Small Things"
Miriam Peretz loves this song so much because it emphasizes the importance of appreciating the small things in life, like a smile, a hug, or a kind word. She highlights that we need to learn to appreciate what we have and face difficult situations with hope for a better future.

"Miriam Peretz on Channel 14"
In an interview with Channel 14, Miriam Peretz shares her pain over the fact that the public discourse is primarily focused on politics, while the memory of Israel's fallen soldiers is being forgotten. She speaks about the soldiers who went into battle together, without differences, with the goal of protecting our home, and calls for a discourse of brotherhood and solidarity.
